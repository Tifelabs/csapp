        \textbf{\small PRINCIPLE:}\\
        For $C$ variable $x$ and $k$ with an Unsigned values $x$ and $k$, such that
        $0 \leq k < w$, the $C$ expression $x>>k$ yields the value $[x/2^k]$

        \textbf{\small DERIVATION}: Unsigned Division by the power of $2$\\
        Let $x$ be the Unsigned integer represented by bit pattern 
        $[x_{w-1},x_{w-2}, \ldots, x_0]$ and let $k$ be in the range $0\leq k < w$. Let
        $x'$ be the Unsigned number with $w-k$ bit representation $[x_{w-1}, x_{w-2},\ldots, x_k]$ and 
        let $x''$ be the unsigned number with $k$ bit representation $[x_{k-1},\ldots, x_0]$ We can 
        therefore see that $x=2^kx' + x''$ and that $0 \leq x'' < 2^k$. It therefore follows that
        $[x/2^k] = x'$
        \bigskip

        \begin{center}
           Performing a logical right shift of bit vector $[x_{w-1}, x_{w-2},\ldots,x_0]$ by $k$ yields
