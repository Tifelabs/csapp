        \textbf{\small PRINCIPLE:} Two's complement negation\\
        For $x$ in range $Tmin_w \leq \ TMax_w$, Its two's complement negation $-^t_wx$
        is given by the formula

        \[
            -^t_wx = {\begin{cases}
                Tmin_w, & x = Tmin_w\\
                -x, & x > Tmin_w
            \end{cases}}
        \]

        \textbf{\small DERIVATION:} Two's complement negation\\
        Observe that $Tmin_w + Tmin_w = -2^{w-1} + -2^{w-1} = -2^w$ This would cause
        negative overflow, and hence $Tmin_w + ^t_w Tmin_w = -2^w + 2^w = 0$ For values
        of $x$ such that $x > TMin_w$, the value - $x$ can also be represented as a $w-bit$ 
        two's complement number and their sum will be $-x+x = 0$
    \end{flushleft}
